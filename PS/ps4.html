These are our notes on planning the Translation Community (mejelly.com) service's software structure.

Content
Potential content available from the site:
Raw articles in English that are not yet translated.
Completely or partially translated articles.
Comments for each translation phrase or sentence.


As detailed in planning, we hope to provide automatic translation from English to Thai in order to help simplify the process for the community members. They will then only need to correct the errors in automatic translation.

Technology
Web Server : Apache2
Scripting Language : Ruby (Ruby on Rails)
Database : PostgreSQL

The basic information processing requirements:
User model: storing names, email addresses, encrypted password, registered date, and role (admin, member).
There are 3 variations of content:
The raw article
The translations
The comments

The raw articles are the original English documents created by a member. We also maintain the record of the source (URL) of the article, timestamp, and so on. The translations are the small chunks of the original article translated into Thai. Lastly, we also maintain the remarks from the community members regarding the quality of the translations. Each of these contents will have the associated user detail.
