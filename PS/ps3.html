1. Answer the following questions:
(a) What will people be able learn about in the community that you’re building? Alternatively, if you are automating some collaborative workﬂow, what work will people accomplish with your system?
Through this community people will collaboratively translate articles from various sources. This will help grow the translation community.
(b) What do you want people to say about your service after a visit?
We want people to say that this is an intuitive and reliable platform for translating documents.
(c) What are the relevant distinct user classes?
On the basis of privilege we will have two user classes:
1. Visitors : These are the users who do not register in the community and are just the site visitors. They do not have access to the translation features i.e they cannot create or edit any translation document. They can only read the translated contents.
2. Administrators :They have complete access to the system. He/She can delete documents and even disable user accounts.


And on the basis of the types of users, our community will have:
1. Tech enthusiasts who are eager to share exciting contents to Thai communities
2. Bloggers who want important articles and blog posts to made available to larger audience
3. Translators
4. Academians
(d) What should a user on a smartphone be able to do?
The user on a smartphone should be able to access all major features such as registration, authentication and translation.

5. Evaluate online alternatives. Find the best existing online communities in your subject area. Note how closely they conform to the six elements of sustainability (magnet content, collaboration, searching and browsing capabilities, delegated moderation, means of removing bozos, and means for extending the community. Also write down anything strikingly good or bad about the registration process and the mechanisms of collaboration, e.g., in discussion forums, comments on articles, and chat rooms. Look for voice and mobile interfaces. If present, try them out. Look for evidence of personalization and direct controls over preferences.
The best alternative we have come accorss is Transifex (https://www.transifex.com/). It is a commercial translation platform. It allows users to translate documents and also provides quality check feature at different sections of the translated content.

Magnet content would be the text source from famous partner and organization. E.g. Coursera, edX. It has the searching and browsing capabilities such as search by project name, search for people speaking specific language etc. We did not come across any moderation feature or bozo removal in the site. Transifex extends its community through business partners like Coursera and they also provide open source program as free usage for open source project (e.g. Django). The site lacks clarity in its interface. It takes several steps to get to the actual content that your are looking for. As a new user of transifex, it took us an unexpected amount of effort to evaluate the site. We did not experience personalization in the platform.
